Medical Exemption Information

Medical exemptions follow a three-step process:

Step One: Secure your Medical Exemption

You must receive documentation from a "medical provider" that grants an exemption which clearly indicates the name and date of birth of your child; no further explanation is required.

The medical provider does NOT need to be a pediatrician or medical doctor.

The medical provider definition is outlined in Title 39 of the Idaho Statute, and includes the following health care professionals listed in Title 54:

  • Psychologists
  • Nurses
  • Speech Pathologists
  • Audiologists
  • Respiratory Therapists
  • Dentists
  • Orthodontists
  • Chiropractors
  • Optometrists
  • Pharmacists
  • Physical Therapists
  • Acupuncturists
  • Nutritionists
  • Massage Therapists
  • Athletic Trainers
  • Midwifes
  • Physicians
  • Physician Assistants

Again, the medical provider need not be a pediatrician. They need only fall into one of above categories and be currently licensed by the State of Idaho.

When contacting the provider, be clear that you're seeking a mask exemption letter. You may be required to pay examination fees. Let them know CDH guidelines indicate that an exemption can be granted on either "medical or mental health" needs, but no formal explanation is required in the document itself and they incur no liability for their evaluation per Title 39.

If you have already secured an exemption, please let us know at exemption@westadaparents.com and we'll add your provider to a list so others can contact them as well.

Step Two: File your Exemption with the School Nurse

Your exemption can be filed via email with the School Nurse to take immediate effect. Simply send the nurse an email with your exemption attached, and that's that. Here is the list of current school nurses and their contact information.

Step Three: Send your Child to School Mask-Free with a Notice

This step is purely optional, but will assist your child in navigating the school environment itself.

In Elementary School it's a good idea to simply send a note to the teacher in advance indicating that you have a Medical Exemption on file with the school nurse.

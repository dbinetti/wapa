Medical Exemption Information

Medical exemptions follow a three-step process:

Step One: Secure your Medical Exemption

You must receive documentation from a "medical provider" that grants an exemption which clearly indicates the name and date of birth of your child; no further explanation is required.

Meaning: the medical provider does NOT need to be a medical doctor.

The medical provider definition is outlined in Title 39 of the Idaho Statute, and includes the following health care professionals listed in Title 54:

  • Psychologists
  • Nurses
  • Speech Pathologists
  • Audiologists
  • Respiratory Therapists
  • Dentists
  • Orthodontists
  • Chiropractors
  • Optometrists
  • Pharmacists
  • Physical Therapists
  • Acupuncturists
  • Nutritionists
  • Massage Therapists
  • Athletic Trainers
  • Midwifes
  • Physicians
  • Physician Assistants

Again, the medical provider need not be a pediatrician or MD. They need only fall into one of above categories and be currently licensed by the State of Idaho. Medical exemptions have already been accepted from chiropractors, nurses, and child psychologists without incident.

When contacting the provider, be clear that you're seeking a mask exemption letter. You may be required to have an in-person examination and pay for the visit. Let the provider know that CDH guidelines indicate that an exemption can be granted on either "medical or mental health" needs, but no formal explanation is required in the document itself and they need not justify their evaluation with anyone.

If you have already secured an exemption, please let us know at exemption@westadaparents.com. We're building a list that we can share with others, and success stories are always great social proof!

Step Two: File your Exemption with the School Nurse

Your exemption can be filed via email with the School Nurse to take immediate effect. Simply send the nurse an email with your exemption attached, and that's that. This step is purely optional, but will assist your child in navigating the school environment itself.

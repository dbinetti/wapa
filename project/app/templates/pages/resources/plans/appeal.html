West Ada School District Quarantine Appeal Process

Following is the official quarantine appeal process as related to the West Ada Parents Association by Superintendent Bub:

We've received many reports of students being sent home for ten days despite remaining masked at school because they were on file as having "opted-out" of the mandate. However, the Superintendent clarified that the opt-out itself is not the criterion for quarantine; the criterion for avoiding quarantine is proper and consistent mask-wearing during close contact (see definitions.)

Example: many students who officially opted-out of the mandate chose to wear their mask in class anyway (sometimes as a personal choice, sometimes in response to social pressure, etc.) These students are not subject to the quarantine. Inversely, many students who did not officially opt-out of the mandate yet are not wearing masks are subject to the quarantine. Again, it's not about what's in PowerSchool, it's about what's on the face.

If your child was sent home as a result of close contact exposure, and he/she wore the mask during the period of exposure, you should:

  • First, notify the Principal that your student was in fact masked and ask for an official quarantine release. If the Principal's response is unsatisfactory, then, and only then, you may,
  • Escalate to your Regional Director. The Regional Directors are listed below by school.

Please note that disagreement with the quarantine policy itself is not sufficient reason to contact the Principal/Regional Director. It is appropriate to ask who made the contact tracing assessment and how it was determined, but do not abuse this process if you know that your student was in fact not masked in class.

If you have other incidents or concerns you wish to share, please send them to support@westadaparents.com and we'll do everything we can to investigate, answer, or advocate on your behalf.

Note: In most cases data are only available as cumulative totals, and as such the rates themseleves are likely overstated. (For example, the CDC national rates of student hospitalization are a seven-day moving average, while the Idaho rate is a cumulative rate for the entire two-year period. As such, the actual Idaho rate is probably closer to .00000001 than .0003)

To provide the most acccurate data, we'd appreciate it if any of our community health partners (such as Central District Health or Primary Health, etc.) would provide time-series data on hospitalization and mortality in order to conduct a proper cohort analysis. Given their ability to provide accurate and timely data on case counts, vaccination and testing, this shouldn't be too burdensome.
